WORK, ENERGY, POWER & MACHINES ‚Äî WRITING NOTES

1) Work

Definition: Work is the product of force and distance moved in the direction of the force.

Formula: \( W = F \times d \)

SI Unit: Joule (J). \( 1 \text{ J} = 1 \text{ Nm} \).

Note: No work is done if there is no movement or if movement is perpendicular to the force (e.g., carrying a bag while walking horizontally).

Worked Example (Stone Mason)

A stone mason lifts a stone of mass 15 kg through a height of 2.0 m.

  • Force = Weight = \( mg = 15 \times 10 = 150 \text{ N} \)
  • Distance = \( 2.0 \text{ m} \)
  • Work = \( 150 \times 2 = \mathbf{300 \text{ J}} \)
2) Energy

Definition: Energy is the ability or capacity to do work. It exists in renewable (solar, wind, water) and non-renewable (fossils, nuclear) forms.

Law of Conservation of Energy: Energy can neither be created nor destroyed but can only be transformed from one form to another.

(a) Gravitational Potential Energy (P.E)

Energy possessed by a body due to its position (height).

\( P.E. = mgh \)

(b) Elastic Potential Energy

Energy stored in a stretched or compressed spring.

\( \text{Work done} = \text{Average Force} \times \text{Extension} \)

\( P.E. = \frac{1}{2}Fe \)

Since \( F = ke \) (Hooke's Law), \( P.E. = \frac{1}{2}ke^2 \)

(c) Kinetic Energy (K.E)

Energy possessed by a body due to its motion.

\( K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \)

Work-Energy Theorem: Work done in stopping a moving body = Kinetic Energy lost.

Example: A car of mass 1250 kg at 20 m/s is brought to rest.

  • Work done = \( \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 1250 \times 20^2 = \mathbf{250,000 \text{ J}} \)
3) Power

Definition: The rate of doing work (or rate of energy conversion).

\( \text{Power} = \frac{\text{Work done}}{\text{Time taken}} \)

Also: \( P = \text{Force} \times \text{Velocity} \) (since \( \frac{d}{t} = v \))

SI Unit: Watt (W). \( 1 \text{ W} = 1 \text{ J/s} \).

4) Machines: Terms & Definitions

A machine is a device that enables work to be done more easily or conveniently.

  • Mechanical Advantage (M.A): Ratio of Load to Effort. \( M.A = \frac{L}{E} \)
  • Velocity Ratio (V.R): Ratio of distance moved by Effort (\( d_E \)) to distance moved by Load (\( d_L \)). \( V.R = \frac{d_E}{d_L} \)
  • Efficiency (\( \eta \)): Ratio of Work Output to Work Input expressed as a percentage.

Formula linking terms: \( \text{Efficiency} = \frac{M.A}{V.R} \times 100\% \)

Why is efficiency always less than 100%?

  1. Work is done against friction in moving parts.
  2. Work is done to lift heavy parts of the machine itself (e.g. the lower pulley block).
5) Specific Machines (VR Formulas)

You must memorize the V.R formula for each machine type.

A. Levers

\( V.R = \frac{\text{Effort Arm}}{\text{Load Arm}} \)

B. Wheel and Axle

\( V.R = \frac{\text{Radius of Wheel (R)}}{\text{Radius of Axle (r)}} \)

C. Inclined Plane

\( V.R = \frac{\text{Length of plane (L)}}{\text{Vertical height (h)}} = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} \)

D. Screw / Screw Jack

\( V.R = \frac{\text{Circumference of handle}}{\text{Pitch}} = \frac{2\pi R}{\text{pitch}} \)

E. Gears

\( V.R = \frac{\text{No. of teeth in Driven wheel}}{\text{No. of teeth in Driver wheel}} \)

F. Pulleys

  • Single Fixed Pulley: V.R = 1
  • Single Movable Pulley: V.R = 2
  • Block and Tackle: V.R = Number of ropes supporting the load.

G. Hydraulic Press

\( V.R = \frac{\text{Area of Load Piston}}{\text{Area of Effort Piston}} = \frac{R^2}{r^2} \)

Essential Formulas

  • \( W = F \times d \)
  • \( K.E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \)
  • \( P.E = mgh \)
  • \( P.E_{\text{elastic}} = \frac{1}{2}ke^2 \)
  • \( \text{Power} = \frac{W}{t} = F \times v \)
  • \( M.A = \frac{L}{E} \)
  • \( \text{Efficiency} = \frac{M.A}{V.R} \times 100\% \)

Common KCSE Explanations

  • Why efficiency < 100%: Energy is lost overcoming friction and lifting moving parts of the machine.
  • M.A vs Load: M.A increases with load (friction becomes less significant relative to load).
  • Efficiency vs Load: Efficiency increases with load initially, then becomes constant.
